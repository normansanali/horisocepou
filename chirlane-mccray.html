Chirlane McCray is an American writer, poet, and political figure. She is the wife of New York City Mayor Bill de Blasio and the First Lady of New York City. She is the first African-American woman to serve in this role. McCray was born in Springfield, Massachusetts, and raised in the nearby town of Longmeadow. She attended Wellesley College, where she earned a degree in English and French literature. After college, she moved to New York City and worked as a journalist and editor for Essence magazine. McCray met Bill de Blasio in 1991, and the two married in 1994. They have two children, Dante and Chiara. McCray has been an advocate for mental health awareness and has spoken publicly about her own struggles with depression. She is also a vocal advocate for women's rights and LGBTQ rights. McCray has written several books, including a memoir, "A Woman Like Me," and a collection of poetry, "The Meaning of Chirlane." She is also a contributing editor for the magazine "The Nation."

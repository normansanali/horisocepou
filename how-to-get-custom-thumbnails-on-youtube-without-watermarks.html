How to get custom thumbnails on Youtube without watermarks

 Posted on August 23, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  349 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Custom thumbnails are an essential part of any successful YouTube channel. They help your videos stand out in search results and give viewers a preview of what to expect from your content. However, if you don't have the resources or skills to create your own custom thumbnail, you may be tempted to use a stock image or a free thumbnail generator. Unfortunately, these options often come with watermarks, which can be distracting and unprofessional.

Luckily, there are several ways to get custom thumbnails on YouTube without watermarks. Here are a few options:
  • Use YouTube's built-in thumbnail editor

YouTube has a built-in thumbnail editor that allows you to create custom thumbnails using stills from your video. To access this editor, go to your video manager and select the video you want to add a thumbnail to. Then, click on the "Edit" button and select "Custom thumbnail." From there, you can choose a still from your video or upload your own image. Once you've selected your thumbnail, click "Save changes" and your custom thumbnail will be live on your video.

  • Use a free image editing tool

If you want more control over your custom thumbnail design, you can use a free image editing tool like Canva or PicMonkey. These tools allow you to create custom designs using templates, graphics, and text. Once you've created your thumbnail, simply upload it to your video as described in option 1.

  • Hire a freelance designer

If you want a truly professional custom thumbnail, consider hiring a freelance designer. Sites like Fiverr and Upwork have thousands of designers who specialize in YouTube thumbnails. You can browse their portfolios, read reviews, and hire the designer that best fits your needs and budget. Once you've received your custom thumbnail, upload it to your video using option 1.

By using these strategies, you can create custom thumbnails for your YouTube videos without any distracting watermarks. Whether you choose to use YouTube's built-in editor, a free image editing tool, or a freelance designer, custom thumbnails are a great way to improve your channel's visual appeal and attract more viewers.

All To Know About Cai Xukun

Kun was born on August 2, 1998, in Wenzhou, Zhejiang, but spent a portion of his early years with his grandparents in Huaihua, Hunan.

He later lived in Shenzhen, Guangdong.

He participated in numerous essay writing contests while in elementary school and was selected to serve as the principal’s assistant and president of the student union.

Kun was also chosen to run for TFBoys, but he chose to concentrate on his studies instead because his parents believed he was too young to be in the entertainment industry.

He relocated to Shenzhen after graduation to finish junior high school at Nashan Experimental High School.

He attended Grace Brethren High School in Simi Valley, California, when he was about 16 years old.

Career Exploits

Kun made his debut on October 18, 2016, as a member of SWIN and its sub-unit SWIN-S, following his participation in the first and second seasons of the Chinese reality series Super Idol.

He left the group and Yihai Entertainment, took part in the reality competition Idol Producer on iQiyi, won, and made his debut on April 6, 2018, as the leader/center of the transient Chinese boy group Nine Percent.

From 2020 until 2022, he was a cast member of the variety program Keep Running.

The majority of Kun’s solo compositions are original, such as “Wait Wait Wait” and his debut EP “1”.

Every song on the EP reached the top of the Chinese music charts, making it a commercial triumph.

The opening tune, “Pull Up,” broke ten records, climbed to the top of four QQ Music charts, and held the top spot on Billboard China’s V Chart for eight weeks.

“Wait Wait Wait” debuted at number one on QQ Music’s New Tune list and Popular Music list for 10 weeks at the time of its release in 2018 and was named Asia’s hottest tune for 4 straight weeks.

On January 10, 2019, the Jamaican Embassy in Shanghai, China, formally appointed Kun the Goodwill Ambassador and Outstanding Young Leader for China and Jamaica.

The “Kun ONE North America/U.K. Tour 2019” was Kun’s first solo tour, which he said would begin in early April 2019.

On July 26, the day after the tour came to an end, Kun released his second EP, “Young,” which includes the songs “Young” and “Blindfolded”.

The EP was warmly received upon release, selling 110,000 copies in a week, earning him the title of “King of Digital Singles” of 2019.
